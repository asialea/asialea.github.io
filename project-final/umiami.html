CCS Data Scholars

During my Junior year, I interned at the University of Miami Center for Computational Science. I spent the semester making revisions and creating new activities for the CCS Data Scholars program. The Data Scholars program is a summer program that exposes middle and high school students to careers in Computational Science. The scholars develop their command-line skills while exercising their creativity through playing with geospatial, image, and text data. I strongly believe that early exposure is the key to bringing more students into tech. Working on this project was a step outside of my comfort zone. With the guidance of my mentor, I was able to conduct research on STEM education and learn how to manipulate data using R.

AAFA: Associative Affinity Factor Analysis for Bot Detection and Stance Classification in Twitter

My first research experience at UM was with the College of Computer Engineering. The goal was to build an automated system for bot detection using Associative Affinity Factor Analysis framework and automatically infer stance of users to see if they were a Clinton or Trump supporter. Through this research I was introduced to the concept of machine learning. I learned how to gather tweet data using the Twitter API to conduct bot detection and sentiment analysis. I used python to clean the data before feeding it to the classification models.
